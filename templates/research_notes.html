Research Notes

Project Overview

This project explores human and AI performance in temporal pattern recognition tasks. The research focuses on understanding how different agents process and identify statistical patterns that emerge over time.

Current Findings

  • Humans show consistent improvement over multiple attempts
  • LLMs demonstrate strong pattern recognition capabilities
  • Response time variations between humans and AI are significant
  • Statistical biases are detected with varying accuracy rates

Methodology

The study employs a controlled experimental design where participants (both human and AI) are presented with sequences of colored cues across multiple rounds. One quadrant is programmatically biased, and success is measured by correct identification of this bias.

Future Directions

Upcoming research will focus on:

  • Extended pattern complexity analysis
  • Cross-model comparative studies
  • Temporal reasoning benchmarks
  • Human-AI collaborative approaches
